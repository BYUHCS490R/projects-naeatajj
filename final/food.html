Food & Local Favorites

Trying local food is one of my favorite parts of living on Oahu. Poke bowls after the beach, hot malasadas on a drive home, or a big plate of loco moco after a long day all feel like small celebrations.

The table and photos on this page highlight five dishes that visitors ask about the most. Instead of giving exact prices for one restaurant, the table shows general price ranges and the types of places where you can usually find each food.

Popular Oahu Foods and Where to Try Them
Food Name Best Place to Try It* Typical Price Range
Poke Poke shops or grocery store poke counters $10–$18 per bowl
Malasadas Local bakeries and malasada trucks $2–$4 per piece
Laulau Plate lunch spots and Hawaiian food restaurants $12–$20 per plate
Loco Moco Diners and plate lunch cafes $12–$18 per plate
Shave Ice Shave ice stands in beach towns and neighborhoods $5–$10 per serving

*Instead of naming specific businesses, this table focuses on the kinds of places where you can usually find each dish. You can connect this with your own favorite shops or new discoveries later.

What These Dishes Are Like

Poke is diced raw fish seasoned with soy sauce, onions, and other flavors. Malasadas are fluffy Portuguese-style doughnuts rolled in sugar.

Laulau is a traditional Hawaiian dish where meat is wrapped in taro leaves and cooked until tender. Loco moco stacks rice, a hamburger patty, fried egg, and gravy into one comfort-food plate. Shave ice is finely shaved ice topped with bright syrups—perfect after a hot beach day.
