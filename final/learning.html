Learning About Portrait Photography

Use this page to explain what you learned throughout your research for the project. Each section should have a reference next to the information you share.

Posing and Lighting for Family Photos

Photographers often like to shoot during “golden hour” because the light is soft and flattering. It’s the time right after sunrise or before sunset. They also recommend having families move a little or use easy prompts so the photos look more natural.

Reference: Crabapple Photography – Must-Have Poses for Outdoor Family Photos

Improving Portrait Skills

Good portraits often come from focusing on the eyes, using gentle light, and communicating with the person in front of the camera. These simple habits help them feel comfortable and look natural.

Reference: Adobe – Portrait Photography Tips

Using Images Legally

If you use an image you didn’t take, make sure to follow the copyright or Creative Commons license and give proper credit. It protects the photographer and also protects you.

Reference: Creative Commons – About CC Licenses

Finding Free-to-Use Photos

Some sites have photos you can use for free because they’re covered by Creative Commons or public domain licenses. They’re helpful for things like backgrounds or sample images, but make sure the license allows it and always give credit.

Reference: CCGA Library – Creative Commons & Public Domain Images
