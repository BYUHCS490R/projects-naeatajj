1. What is the parent-child relationship in HTML, and why is it important in Flex and Grid?

In HTML, the parent-child relationship describes how elements are nested. The parent element contains other elements, and those inside it are the children. This structure is essential when using Flexbox and Grid because the parent controls the layout behavior (for example, `display: flex` or `display: grid`), while the children become flexible or grid items that respond to those layout rules.
